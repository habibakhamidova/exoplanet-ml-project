Project Summary of Exoplanet Transit Detection using Kepler Data
Goal
I wanted to test how well the BLS (Box Least Squares) method works on real Kepler data, not clean textbook examples.
What I did
I downloaded Kepler light curve data and tried to detect exoplanet transits using BLS under different configurations. Even when the transit was clearly visible in the data, BLS often failed to find the correct period.
Then I extracted simple features like transit depth and duration and used a Random Forest model as a basic comparison.
Results
The ML model reached about 83% accuracy on a small test set. The results are not perfect because weak signals and noise can look similar.
Conclusion
BLS is not reliable on short or noisy datasets without additional processing. Simple ML features can help, but they are also limited.
Limitations
Small dataset (20 targets), only two features used, and duration estimation is approximate.
